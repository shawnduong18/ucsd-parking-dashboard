About This Dashboard

What is this?

This dashboard visualizes parking occupancy survey data collected by UCSD Transportation Services across 20 academic quarters from Fall 2019 through Winter 2025–26. It shows how full various types of parking spaces get — by permit type, by individual lot, and by structure — at different times of day and across academic seasons.

How is occupancy measured?

Each quarter, Transportation Services conducts a physical count of occupied and empty parking stalls at 10 hourly snapshots throughout a single survey day (8am–5pm). The occupancy percentage shown is the peak — the busiest single hour of that day — not an all-day average.

What do the permit types mean?

  • A permit — Faculty and staff lots
  • B permit — Student lots
  • S permit — Student parking structures
  • D permit — Daily/visitor pay lots
  • SR — Special Reserved (pre-assigned spaces)
  • Carpool — Designated carpool spaces
  • Electric Vehicle — EV charging stalls
  • Visitor — Pay-by-space visitor parking

Data notes

The 2023–2024 academic year is missing from source files — there is a gap in the dataset between Spring 2023 and Summer 2024. Structure-level data for 2024–2026 is not yet available in this dashboard. Lot naming conventions changed over time; older survey files used codes like (P341-7) which correspond to named structures in newer files.

Coming soon

Future versions of this dashboard will include lot-level trend analysis, a predictive occupancy tool, and real-time data integration.
